I am a historian and education specialist whose work connects the history of education, decolonisation and development with hands-on experience of policy and practice in international organisations, ministries and classrooms.

I am drawn to how education changes over time, and to what the ambitions of earlier reformers can tell us about the choices it faces today. Before my doctoral research at the Geneva Graduate Institute, I worked at the UNESCO Institute for Lifelong Learning in Hamburg, coordinating policy dialogues, workshops and research.

About

Looking at education over time

For more than a decade I have moved between the classroom, the international organisation and the archive. I began as a primary teacher in Manchester, trained in comparative education and international development in Edinburgh, and worked at the UNESCO Institute for Lifelong Learning in Hamburg, coordinating policy dialogues, workshops and research across Africa and Asia.

These experiences now underpin my doctoral research in international history and politics at the Geneva Graduate Institute. I am interested in the weight of expectation education is made to carry, the gap between promise and reality, and the global and transnational forces that shape, over time, what is deemed possible.
