The Reform Cycle

Global promises in retrospect.

Over the past sixty years, learning around the world has been subjected to successive cycles of reform, each revolving around a grand promise: universal primary schooling, decolonisation, Education for All, sustainable development, learning for the knowledge economy, and now AI, to name a few. The Reform Cycle asks the people involved to look back and consider how those promises and reforms were understood in their time. What was believed possible? How did understandings and experiences of learning shift, in expected and unexpected ways? Who was reached and who was neglected? What are the legacies of those reforms today, and are they informing new debates around learning?
