The Reform Cycle

Global promises in retrospect.

Over the past sixty years, learning has been repeatedly reshaped by ambitious reform movements carrying hopes for a better future. From universal schooling and Education for All to today's debates around artificial intelligence, each cycle has carried its own vision of what learning could achieve. The Reform Cycle asks the people who shaped and experienced those moments to look back: what did they believe was possible at the time, and what became of those ambitions?

Through historical conversations with policymakers, practitioners and others who lived these reforms, the series explores how ideas about learning change, how promises are transformed in practice, and lessons from earlier cycles for the ones now emerging.
