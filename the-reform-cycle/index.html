The Reform Cycle

Education from a global and long-term perspective.

Over the past sixty years, education at the global level has moved through successive cycles of reform, each revolving around a grand promise: universal primary schooling, decolonisation, Education for All, sustainable development, learning for the knowledge economy, and now AI, to name a few. The Reform Cycle asks the people caught up in those promises — from the architects and officials to teachers and students — to view these cycles in retrospect and consider what they reveal about the reforms of today and tomorrow. No cycle has ever run its course, no promise has been entirely fulfilled. But what was believed possible at the time? How did understandings and experiences of education shift, for better and worse, in expected and unexpected ways? What remains of those reforms now, and where are we going?
